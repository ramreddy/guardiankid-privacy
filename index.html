GuardianKid — Privacy Policy

Last updated: June 4, 2026

GuardianKid (“the app”, “we”, “us”) is a parental-control and screen-time enforcement app for Android. This policy explains what the app does with your information. The short version: GuardianKid runs entirely on your device. It has no servers of its own, does not require an account, and does not transmit your personal data anywhere.

What data the app processes, and where

GuardianKid is designed to work offline, on-device. The categories below are processed locally and are never uploaded to us (we operate no servers).

Camera / face detection

  • The app uses the front camera with on-device face detection (Google ML Kit) to estimate how close the device is being held to the user’s face, so it can nudge the child to hold it farther away.
  • Camera frames are analysed in memory and discarded immediately after each frame. No photo or video is ever saved, cached, or transmitted.
  • The only value derived from a frame is a single number — the ratio of the detected face’s width to the image width — used to decide “too close”. Faces are not identified, recognised, matched, or stored.

App usage and the active app

  • To enforce blocking and time limits, the app needs to know which app is currently in the foreground. It reads this via the Accessibility Service and Usage Access permission.
  • This information is used only on the device, in real time, to decide whether to show a lock screen. It is not logged off-device or transmitted.

Settings and parent PIN

  • Your settings, schedules, and the parent PIN are stored locally on the device using Android DataStore. The PIN is stored as a salted hash, never in plain text. None of this leaves the device.

Purchases

  • The optional one-time “Premium unlock” is processed by Google Play Billing. We never see or store your payment details — Google handles the transaction. The app only records, locally, whether Premium has been unlocked.

All permissions are used solely for the parental-control features described above.

Crash and diagnostics data

If crash reporting (Google Firebase Crashlytics) is enabled, the app sends anonymous crash reports and diagnostic information (such as the type of crash, device model, OS version, and an app-generated installation identifier) to Google when the app crashes. This is used only to find and fix bugs. It does not include your camera data, the apps you block, your PIN, or any content you create. See Google’s privacy policy: https://policies.google.com/privacy.

What we do NOT do

  • We do not collect, sell, or share your personal data.
  • We do not show ads or use advertising/tracking SDKs.
  • We do not require an account or sign-in.
  • We do not store or upload photos, videos, or audio.
  • We do not track your location.

Children’s privacy

GuardianKid is configured by a parent or guardian and may run on a child’s device. Because all processing happens on the device and nothing is transmitted to us, the app does not knowingly collect personal information from children. The parent controls all settings.

Data retention and deletion

Because data stays on the device, uninstalling the app removes the locally stored data (settings, PIN, Premium flag). Your Google Play purchase record is held by Google and can be managed through your Google account.

Changes to this policy

We may update this policy as the app evolves. The “Last updated” date at the top will reflect any change.
